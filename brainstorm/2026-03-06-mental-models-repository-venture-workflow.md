## Problem Statement

Venture operators and the Chairman AI make hundreds of decisions across the 25-stage workflow — from initial problem identification (Stage 0) through launch and operations. These decisions rely on implicit mental models that are neither documented, consistently applied, nor tracked for effectiveness. This creates three problems:

1. **Inconsistent decision quality** — operators apply mental models haphazardly based on personal experience
2. **No institutional learning** — effective model applications aren't captured or shared across ventures
3. **Cognitive overload** — operators must remember which frameworks apply to which stage context

A formalized mental models repository with AI-powered suggestion, effectiveness tracking, and multi-output delivery would standardize decision-making quality across all ventures.

## Discovery Summary

### Delivery Mechanism
**AI auto-suggests** models based on stage context, venture archetype, and historical effectiveness data. The system proactively surfaces relevant mental models at each decision point rather than requiring operators to browse or search.

### Output Types (All Three)
1. **Guided exercises** — Interactive walkthroughs that apply a model to the current venture context
2. **Scored evaluations** — Quantitative assessments using model-specific rubrics (e.g., Blue Ocean Strategy canvas scored 0-10 per dimension)
3. **Structured artifacts** — Exportable deliverables (competitor matrices, risk registers, decision logs) that persist in the venture record

### Scope: Stages 0-12 + Operations
- **THE TRUTH** (Stages 1-5): Problem validation, market reality, customer discovery
- **THE ENGINE** (Stages 6-9): Business model, revenue mechanics, growth strategy
- **THE IDENTITY** (Stages 10-12): Brand, positioning, narrative
- **Operations** (Post-Stage 25): Ongoing optimization, scaling decisions, portfolio management

### LEO Integration
- Venture-only scope (not LEO Protocol itself)
- Effectiveness tracking correlated with venture outcomes (stage progression, revenue, retention)

### Learning System
- **Track & rank** effectiveness correlated with venture outcomes
- Models that consistently correlate with positive stage progression rank higher in suggestions
- Poor-performing models deprioritize over time (not removed — may work in different archetypes)

## Analysis

### Arguments For

1. **Formalizes implicit knowledge** — Stage 0 already has unnamed mental models embedded in path-router.js and chairman-review.js. Formalizing them creates shared vocabulary, makes the system auditable, and enables reuse across ventures.

2. **AI auto-suggestion reduces cognitive load** — Venture operators don't need to know all 43 models. The system surfaces the 3-5 most relevant models based on stage, venture archetype, and historical effectiveness. This scales decision quality without scaling training.

3. **Effectiveness tracking creates proprietary intelligence** — As ventures progress through stages, the system learns which models actually correlate with success. This calibrated intelligence becomes a competitive moat — no competitor has venture-outcome-correlated model rankings.

4. **Clean integration path** — Stage 0 synthesis engine (components 11-14) already processes structured analyses. Mental model outputs slot into this existing pipeline. The Stage 0 schema already supports structured artifacts.

5. **Operations coverage fills a real gap** — The current 25-stage workflow ends at launch. Post-launch optimization, scaling decisions, and portfolio management have no structured framework today.

### Arguments Against

1. **"Sophisticated rationalization engine" risk** — The Challenger's strongest concern: mental models can be used to justify pre-existing conclusions rather than genuinely challenge them. Auto-suggestion based on context could confirm biases rather than surface uncomfortable truths. **Mitigation**: Include "adversarial" models (Inversion, Survivorship Bias) in every suggestion set.

2. **Single-venture validation weakness** — With only PortraitPro AI as an active venture, effectiveness rankings will be based on n=1 data. Statistical significance requires 5+ ventures through similar stages. **Mitigation**: Phase 1 focuses on structure, not ranking. Rankings become meaningful as the venture portfolio grows.

3. **Investment vs. proven demand** — 18-23 person-weeks for MVP is substantial. No operator has explicitly requested mental model tooling. **Mitigation**: Pragmatist's phased approach (8-10 weeks for Stage 0 + THE TRUTH only) reduces initial commitment.

## Team Perspectives

### Challenger
- **Blind Spots**: Chairman already has unnamed mental models baked into entry path routing and synthesis scoring; market assumptions are embedded in Stage 0 path selection without being surfaced as explicit mental models; outcome tracking has a measurement problem (is it the model or the operator?)
- **Assumptions at Risk**: More structured information does not necessarily equal better decisions; effectiveness ranking could be corrupted by survivorship bias (models used on winning ventures rank high regardless of causation); auto-suggestion may kill novel ventures by always routing to "proven" models
- **Worst Case**: A sophisticated rationalization engine that gives operators false confidence. Ventures that should be killed survive longer because "the models said it's fine." 10x opportunities are filtered out because they don't score well on conventional frameworks.

### Visionary
- **Opportunities**: "Mental Model as Moat" — methodology differentiation that competitors can't easily replicate; Cross-venture learning loop where insights compound across the portfolio; AI agent scaling where Chairman uses model templates to evaluate more ventures in parallel
- **Synergies**: Skill Assessment System pattern reuse (existing effectiveness tracking infrastructure); Stage 0 synthesis engine integration (components 11-14 already process structured analyses); Research Department intelligence injection (domain knowledge informs model selection); Narrative Risk framework backbone (models feed into risk scoring)
- **Upside Scenario**: Chairman scales from 3-4 to 8-10 concurrent ventures with consistent decision quality. The calibrated model effectiveness data becomes a sellable asset or licensing opportunity. EHG's venture methodology becomes the "Moneyball of startups."

### Pragmatist
- **Feasibility**: 6.5/10
- **Resource Requirements**: 18-23 person-weeks MVP total; 4 new database tables; ~$180-240K equivalent developer time; Phase 1 alone: 8-10 weeks, 12-15 models, Stage 0 + THE TRUTH only
- **Constraints**: Single venture provides weak validation signal; context-aware routing is complex (stage + archetype + history = large feature space); three output types (exercises, evaluations, artifacts) multiply implementation scope
- **Recommended Path**:
  - **Phase 1 (8-10 weeks)**: 12-15 core models, Stage 0 + THE TRUTH, guided exercises only, basic effectiveness logging
  - **Phase 2 (6-8 weeks)**: Expand to THE ENGINE + THE IDENTITY, add scored evaluations, build suggestion algorithm
  - **Phase 3 (4-6 weeks)**: Operations coverage, structured artifacts, full effectiveness ranking, cross-venture analytics

### Proposed Database Schema (from Pragmatist)

```sql
-- Core model definitions
mental_models (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  name TEXT NOT NULL,
  slug TEXT UNIQUE,
  category TEXT,  -- decision, market, psychology, growth, framework
  description TEXT,
  core_concept TEXT,
  applicable_stages INTEGER[],  -- [0, 1, 2, 3]
  applicable_archetypes TEXT[],  -- venture archetypes
  difficulty_level TEXT,  -- beginner, intermediate, advanced
  exercise_template JSONB,
  evaluation_rubric JSONB,
  artifact_template JSONB,
  created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
)

-- Track which models were applied to which ventures at which stages
mental_model_applications (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  model_id UUID REFERENCES mental_models(id),
  venture_id UUID,
  stage_number INTEGER,
  applied_by TEXT,  -- 'ai_suggestion' | 'manual_selection'
  exercise_output JSONB,
  evaluation_score NUMERIC,
  artifact_data JSONB,
  operator_rating INTEGER,  -- 1-5 usefulness
  created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT NOW()
)

-- Aggregate effectiveness scores
mental_model_effectiveness (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  model_id UUID REFERENCES mental_models(id),
  stage_number INTEGER,
  venture_archetype TEXT,
  application_count INTEGER DEFAULT 0,
  avg_operator_rating NUMERIC,
  stage_progression_correlation NUMERIC,  -- -1.0 to 1.0
  revenue_correlation NUMERIC,
  retention_correlation NUMERIC,
  composite_effectiveness_score NUMERIC,
  last_calculated_at TIMESTAMPTZ
)

-- Archetype affinity (which models work best for which venture types)
model_archetype_affinity (
  id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  model_id UUID REFERENCES mental_models(id),
  archetype TEXT,
  affinity_score NUMERIC,  -- 0.0 to 1.0
  sample_size INTEGER,
  confidence_level TEXT,  -- low, medium, high
  updated_at TIMESTAMPTZ
)
```

### Synthesis
- **Consensus Points**: Phase 1 MVP should target Stage 0 + THE TRUTH with limited model set; effectiveness tracking is the long-term differentiator but needs careful anti-bias design; existing Stage 0 synthesis engine is the integration point
- **Tension Points**: Auto-suggestion as scaling enabler (Visionary) vs. bias amplifier (Challenger); ambitious 8-10 venture scaling (Visionary) vs. conservative start (Pragmatist); formalizing models may constrain creative exploration (Challenger) vs. providing guardrails (Pragmatist)
- **Composite Risk**: **Medium** — Challenger concerns are valid but mitigable through phased approach with effectiveness tracking and mandatory adversarial model inclusion

## Open Questions

1. **Adversarial model guarantee**: Should every suggestion set include at least one "adversarial" model (Inversion, Survivorship Bias) to counter confirmation bias?
2. **Operator override**: When AI suggests models, can operators dismiss all suggestions and select manually? (Recommended: yes, with logging)
3. **Cross-venture anonymization**: When effectiveness data spans multiple ventures, how much venture context is shared in rankings?
4. **Operations stage definition**: Post-Stage 25 operations need a formal stage numbering scheme (Stage 26+? or Operations.1, Operations.2?)
5. **Model retirement**: Under what conditions does a model get flagged as "ineffective" and stop being suggested?

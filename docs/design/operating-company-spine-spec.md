## §0 — Purpose and the one-line org model
The operating layer converts a LAUNCHED venture (post-S26 machinery running) into a **governed, self-operating business**: agents run it, gauges prove it, exceptions—and only exceptions—reach the chairman. One line: **EVA is chief-of-staff to the chairman; each venture has one CEO agent accountable for its thesis; VPs are the CEO's task-scoped delegates; the chairman governs by exception through a meeting surface EVA runs.** Everything below is the mechanics of that sentence.

## §1 — STRUCTURAL (failure class inherited: forged approval → authority polarity)
- **S1.1 Roles:** Chairman (human, authority) · EVA (chief-of-staff: cross-venture roll-up, meeting surface, escalation routing — owns NO venture) · Venture-CEO (one per venture: owns the thesis, the gauges, and the iterate-or-kill recommendation) · VPs (CEO-scoped delegates per function — demand, operations, product — instantiated on demand per the mission's expertise-on-demand clause, never standing bureaucracy).
- **S1.2 Authority is BORN-DENIED:** every role's permission set is explicit and default-empty; a capability not granted is a capability absent (the dispatch-polarity fix as an org invariant). A VP cannot hold CEO authority; a CEO cannot hold chairman authority; **no agent may ever write an approval artifact attributed to a higher authority** (the C1/EVA-force-approve class, banned structurally: approval records carry enforced writer identity, not naming conventions).
- **S1.3 Delegation depth is declared:** each grant names its depth (may the CEO re-delegate to a VP?) and its blast-radius ceiling (spend cap, external-send cap, schema-touch = never).
- **S1.4 Inter-role CONTRACTS:** CEO↔VP and EVA↔CEO interfaces are typed message contracts (the typed-kinds lesson): every ask carries reply-class {fire-and-forget | reply-needed | live-handshake}; untyped messages are a lint error, not a silent orphan.

## §2 — OPERATIONAL (failure class: RUN-ORDER lag → trigger architecture)
- **S2.1 The operating rhythm:** each venture declares its recurring activity set (support triage, demand execution, cost watch, gauge review — the O5 loops) with cadence, owner role, and artifact produced. An activity with no owner or no artifact is not an activity — it is theater by definition.
- **S2.2 Trigger architecture — wake-on-actionable, never poll-and-hope:** every loop is either (a) event-driven off a durable typed signal, or (b) scheduled with liveness recording (last-ran + outcome). Directive-class messages are HARD interrupts to their target role, not batch-tick pickups (the drain-invisibility lesson). The unified trigger spec declares **event classes, their subscribers, and a LATENCY BUDGET per class** (a directive consumed in 3 hours met no budget because none existed — the gap-analysis's sharpening, adopted). The three-issuance RUN-ORDER class is the anti-pattern: an order that must be re-sent is a trigger-architecture defect, chargeable to this spec.
- **S2.3 The MEETING SURFACE (the chairman's articulated vision, specified):** (i) **EVA roll-up meeting** — recurring, chairman-facing: per-venture status from provenance-reached gauges ONLY (a gauge with no data renders NO-DATA — never a synthesized number), attention items = the exception queue (§5), decisions-needed presented as groomed decision cards (machine-precheck done, taste-only residue). (ii) **Deep-dive** — chairman requests it; EVA schedules a CALENDAR meeting with the venture's CEO agent; the CEO presents against its thesis and pre-registered kills, VPs attend for their functions. Meetings are the exception surface and the audit surface — every meeting emits a disposition record of what was decided.

## §3 — ECONOMIC (failure class: token-burn throttling → cost ownership)
- **S3.1 Budget authority per role:** each role carries a spend envelope (tokens, infra dollars, external services) granted downward, never assumed; exceeding it is an exception (§5), not a silent overrun. Live-mode money movement (Stripe live keys, domain purchase, ad spend) is chairman-authority always.
- **S3.2 Cost attribution:** every agent-hour and infra-dollar attributes to venture × role × activity — the income-replacement gauge's denominator. An unattributable cost is a finding.
- **S3.3 Objective functions are EXPLICIT (the compensation-analog; the most dangerous dimension to leave implicit):** each role's "better" is written: CEO optimizes thesis-proof rate (Phase-1) then venture economics (Phase-2) — posture-conditioned, never hardcoded; VPs optimize their function's gauge WITHOUT gaming the CEO's (anti-Goodhart pairing: every optimized gauge is paired with a guard gauge); EVA optimizes chairman-attention efficiency (fewer, better exceptions — NOT fewer escalations at any cost, which incentivizes burying: under-escalation is a graded failure, per the Adam-autonomy doctrine, bi-directional).

## §4 — EPISTEMIC (failure classes: stale-board re-asks → grounding; fabricated 15/15 → gauges)
- **S4.1 Grounding per role is INJECTED, not fetched-if-remembered:** each role's correctness-gating knowledge (its venture's thesis + kills, the operating model, its authority grants) is compiled into its working context at wake (the queryable≠consulted rule); the long tail stays queryable.
- **S4.2 Memory continuity:** each role has durable, role-keyed memory surviving session rotation; succession (§6) transfers it. Board/task state binds to SD-1-style disposition rows so a crash-recovered agent resumes from LIVE state, never from a recovered transcript's stale queue (this week's 5×-recurring class, banned at birth).
- **S4.3 Org-level liveness gauges:** every role emits heartbeat + last-work-product; a role that is PID-alive but work-dead is VISIBLE (the wedge-discriminator rule). A dead CEO is an exception within one cadence period, never a silent no-op quarter.
- **S4.4 Learning-speed (the moat, made falsifiable — satellite consumes this hook):** each venture's retro artifacts feed a cross-venture transfer mechanism with a measurable claim: venture N+1 starts from a higher baseline than venture N, evidenced (time-to-stage, defect classes not repeated), not asserted.

## §5 — GOVERNANCE (failure class: decision-binding → audit; the exception model IS govern-by-exception)
- **S5.1 The EXCEPTION MODEL (the spine's heart):** an exception is a typed object {venture, role, class, evidence, decision-needed-by, blast-radius} routed EVA→chairman. The classification is the only-the-chairman-can set applied per-venture: kill/scale/promote decisions, live-money flips, thesis reversals, cross-venture arbitration (shared-capability contention — EVA arbitrates operationally, chairman on conflict), and anything irreversible. Everything else SELF-SERVES within granted authority, logged. Both drift directions are graded: over-escalation burns the chairman; under-escalation is autonomy theater (the bi-directional doctrine applies to every role, not just Adam).
- **S5.2 Audit:** every agent decision above a declared threshold writes a disposition row (SD-1 generalized): who, what, under which grant, evidence pointer. The trail is the accountability mechanism that makes delegation safe to widen over time — autonomy is EARNED per role via clean audit history (the graduated-activation pattern).
- **S5.3 Vigilance ownership (gap 6):** competitive watching is a named EVA-level recurring activity with a real data path (rebuilt per the observed-baseline design — never the retired upsert-theater), feeding CEO reprioritization.

## §6 — LIFECYCLE (failure class: Adam crash-recovery → succession)
- **S6.1 Agent lifecycle:** roles are instantiated from role contracts (the CLAUDE_*.md pattern generalized: contract + grants + objective + gauges), evaluated on their objective function per cadence (the tri-party rubric generalized), and retired/replaced on sustained under-performance — replacement is a chairman-visible event for CEOs, CEO-authority for VPs.
- **S6.2 Mid-operation failure:** a role dying mid-task must not orphan its venture: the successor registers against the SAME role-keyed memory + disposition state (S4.2), inherits open items from LIVE state, and announces the succession on the org lane (the crash-handoff pattern, made a contract requirement).
- **S6.3 Venture-event hooks:** the org executes kill/exit/scale decisions via the O8 machinery — kill runs the pre-registered criteria without renegotiation; exit runs the teardown checklist; the org's roles wind down with the venture (no ghost CEOs — the scheduler-residue lesson).
- **S6.4 The org improves itself:** every role participates in the mutual-feedback loop (what-worked / friction / one-change, the proven cycle format); loop health is EVA-owned; the loop's own channel must be loss-proof (typed kinds + delivery verification — a feedback loop on a lossy channel silently decays).

## §6.5 — Default gap-ownership mapping (from the gap-analysis's pattern paragraph, adopted as the spine's default)
Satellites land on named roles, not on "the org": **VP_GROWTH** owns demand loops + learning-speed transfer (gap 4's operating half); **VP_STRATEGY** owns competitive vigilance (gap 6); **the CEO** owns the vessel/capability-extraction duty (gap 5) and the iterate-or-kill recommendation; **EVA** owns the exception surface, the meeting cadence, and cross-venture arbitration (gaps 2–3's operating surface). Default, chairman-adjustable — but no satellite ships unowned (§0's third invariant).

## Acceptance (mock-distinguishing, per the value-authenticity discipline)
1. A stubbed CEO agent (returns plausible status, does no work) is caught within one cadence by S4.3 liveness + S2.1 artifact-required — mechanically, not by the chairman noticing.
2. A forged-authority write (VP writing a CEO-attributed record) is REJECTED at write time (S1.2), not discovered in audit.
3. The chairman's weekly attention on a healthy venture ≈ one roll-up scan; on a sick venture, exactly the exception cards — measured, because chairman-attention-per-venture is itself a §3.2-attributed, §4.3-gauged quantity.
4. A satellite (e.g. CRM/pipeline) that ships its OWN exception queue or its OWN objective-function definitions instead of consuming §5.1/§3.3 is a §8 boundary violation — caught in review by the registry's consume-not-rebuild rule, before it forks governance.
